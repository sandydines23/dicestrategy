Three things every stake dice strategy needs

Before you touch a bet-progression system, get these fundamentals right. Most bankrolls die from bad sizing, not bad luck.

01

House edge first

Every bitcoin dice game runs on a fixed house edge, typically 1%. No progression changes that number — it only changes how fast your bankroll swings around it.

02

Bankroll units, not bets

Size each wager as a small percentage of your total bankroll (0.5–2%), not a fixed coin amount. This is what actually separates a dice strategy from gambling blind.

03

A stop-loss you respect

Decide your session loss limit before you place a single bet. The best dice strategy is the one you can actually follow when a losing streak hits.

Common dice strategy systems

None of these beat the house edge — they just change your win/loss variance shape. Read the full breakdown in the guide.

SystemHow it worksRisk profile
MartingaleDouble the bet after every lossHigh — fast bankroll depletion on long losing streaks
Reverse MartingaleDouble the bet after every winMedium — protects bankroll, caps upside
D'AlembertIncrease/decrease bet by one unit per roundLow-medium — smoother swings than Martingale
Flat bettingSame bet size every roundLow — most predictable, easiest to bankroll-manage
